Bartending

Bartending is not only my current career, but it is actually a passion of mine. I don't actually drink alcohol, but it is extremely interesting to me. It has been a centerpiece of humanity for as long as history has been recorded. I have a ton of fun finding new ways to combine ingredients and entertain people. I find alcohol interesting because you can find enjoyment in it whether you barely scratch the surface, and enjoy a light beer once a month, or if you dive deep and drink craft cocktails with homemade syrups, shrubs, high-end liquors, or if you like to sip expensive whiskey neat.

Mountain Biking

Mountain Biking is something that I've been interested in since I was a kid, but never really got into it because it is very expensive. In 2019, I was able to save up enough to get a decent mountain bike for around $1,000, and I fell in love It's extremely dangerous and gets your adrenaline going like nothing else I've ever done. However, for those same reasons, I haven't mountain biked much since 2019. I took a few hard falls (luckily without serious injury) and realized how risky it is. Someone like me who works in the service industry, I need my limbs to work properly in order to work. Not only would the hospital bills be super expensive, but paying my other bills would be difficult too. I still will continue to bike for the foreseeable future, but I will have to take it easy.

Bowling

In early 2018 I started working at a bowling alley in bellingham as a side-job while I went to school. I immediately found out that there is a subculture of people who take bowling very seriously. I really enjoy sports and activities where you are competing against yourself, and bowling usually has a handicap system built in. I started bowling in leagues, and fell in love with it. Over the first few league nights, your scores are tracked and averaged out. This average is then used to calculate how many "free points" you get. The purpose of this is so that if you bowl better than you usually do, you will win, even if the person you are bowling against bowls a better game than you, but bowls below their own average. This concept of not needing to beat your opponents, but needing to beat yourself hooked me.
